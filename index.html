I.

About

Karan is a software engineer with an M.S. in Computer Science from the University of Texas at Arlington, where he graduated with a 3.83 GPA and spent two years split between information-security coursework and the Cyber Guard Research Lab.

His interests live where two fields fold over each other — machine learning that has to be trustworthy, and security tooling that has to think. His thesis-adjacent work on BERT vs. LSTMs for sentiment analysis closed at 94.2% accuracy1 and was written up as a paper; his weekend tooling for catching unsafe Python patterns ships as PyWard.

The interesting work happens where two fields fold over each other.

Now he writes software for the food & beverage supply chain at iTradeNetwork2 — the unglamorous, load-bearing kind of software that runs in the background of meals you've already eaten. Outside the keyboard, he chases trails (Moab3, the Grand Canyon, Niagara) and reads more than he probably should.

It's pronounced kah-rahn, by the way.

    Sentiment Analysis

    2023

    BERT vs. LSTMs on the IMDB corpus — a paper.

    AbstractFine-tuned BERT compared against bidirectional LSTM baselines on the IMDB binary corpus.
    MethodsBERT-base, AdamW @ 2e-5, 3 epochs. LSTM with GloVe embeddings.

    2023

    A careful read of CVE-2022-0847.
